## Q1 findings — on-time delivery by state

- National late delivery rate: 8%
- Worst state: Alagoas (AL) at 23.9% — 3x the national average
- Northeast cluster (AL, MA, PI, CE, SE, BA) all above 14% late rate
- Highest absolute late orders: Rio de Janeiro (RJ) with 1,664 late orders
- São Paulo (SP) best among high-volume states at 5.9% despite 40,501 orders
- Amazon region (AM) has longest avg delivery at 25.6 days but low late rate —
  suggests Olist inflates delivery estimates for remote areas


## Q2 findings — seller processing time by state

- Processing times are consistent across all states (0.3–0.7 days avg)
- Seller processing is NOT the primary bottleneck — delays occur downstream
  in shipping and last-mile delivery
- Single seller in MA handles 388 orders with 23.2% late rate —
  highest late rate among high-volume seller states
- AM seller: 66.7% late rate and 2.33 avg review score (smallest sample — 3 orders)
- SP dominates seller volume: 1,764 sellers handling 68,376 orders at 8.8% late rate
- Review scores are remarkably consistent (4.0–4.5) across all states
  regardless of processing speed — confirms processing time alone doesn't
  drive customer satisfaction


## Q3 findings — delivery delay vs review score

- On-time/early orders score consistently between 4.2 and 4.32
- Early delivery beyond 7 days adds almost no satisfaction benefit (4.31 vs 4.32)
- Review scores collapse the moment an order is late:
  - 0-7 days early: 4.20
  - 1-3 days late:  3.77 (-0.43 drop)
  - 4-7 days late:  2.32 (-1.88 drop from on-time)
  - 8-14 days late: 1.74
  - 14+ days late:  1.71
- Score plateaus below 1.75 beyond 8 days late — additional delay
  causes no further satisfaction loss (damage already done)
- Operational implication: preventing any lateness is far more valuable
  than reducing how late an order is once it's already delayed
- 5,025 orders are 4+ days late — these are generating reviews below 2.32
  and actively damaging Olist's marketplace reputation

## Q4 findings — category performance

- Audio has the highest late rate at 12.7% and low review score (3.84)
- Office furniture has the longest avg delivery at 19.9 days — nearly
  double most categories — driving its 3.51 review score (lowest in dataset)
- High volume problem categories:
  - health_beauty: 8,647 orders, 9.1% late (~787 late orders)
  - electronics: 2,517 orders, 9.7% late (~244 late orders)
  - bed_bath_table: 9,272 orders, 8.4% late (~779 late orders)
- Heavy/bulky categories (office_furniture, construction tools) consistently
  show longer delivery times — suggests carrier capacity issues for large items
- food category performs well operationally: 8.7 avg delivery days
  despite being perishable — likely prioritised by carriers


## Q5 findings — monthly order volume and delivery trend

- Olist grew from 265 orders (Oct 2016) to 7,000+ orders/month by 2018 — 25x growth in 18 months
- Three distinct operational stress events identified:

  1. Nov 2017 (Black Friday): orders spike 63% MoM to 7,289
     Late rate: 5.3% → 14.3%, Review score drops to 3.99
     Logistics network unable to absorb demand surge

  2. Feb–Mar 2018: sustained crisis — NOT demand-driven
     Feb late rate: 16.0%, Mar late rate: 21.4% (dataset peak)
     Avg delivery days: 16.4 days in Feb
     Review score: 3.81 (dataset low for a full month)
     Likely cause: carrier or warehouse operational failure

  3. Aug 2018: 10.4% late rate despite fastest avg delivery (7.7 days)
     Likely data artifact — orders not yet delivered when dataset compiled

- Outside crisis periods, late rate stabilises between 3–6%
  suggesting baseline operations are manageable
- Delivery times have improved: 19 days avg (Oct 2016) → 8.9 days (Jul 2018)
  as network matured and expanded
